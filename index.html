The neighbourhood

Where real Paris still lives

Montmartre and South Pigalle (SoPi) are two of the last Parisian neighbourhoods where artisans, bakers, and vignerons still work the way their grandparents did. This is where we walk, taste, and linger.

  • Rue des Martyrs — the city's best food street
  • Cave de la Fontaine — 200-year-old wine cellar
  • Fromagerie du Martyr — artisan cheeses since 1962
  • Vignes de Montmartre — the last Paris vineyard
  • Boulangerie du Soleil — secret croissant spot

About Thomas

A Parisian who believes food is the best conversation starter

I grew up in the 9th, eating Comté at the corner fromagerie and arguing about Burgundy vs Bordeaux with my father. After years working in hospitality and earning my WSET certification, I decided to turn that obsession into something people could share.

Every tour I lead is different — because every group is different. I adapt the narrative to what makes you curious. No scripts, no rushed stops, no tourist traps.
